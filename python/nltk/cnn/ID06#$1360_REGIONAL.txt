 Islamabad, Pakistan (CNN) -- A suicide bomber targeting military vehicles killed eight people in northwestern Pakistan Monday, Pakistani military officials told CNN. The blast erupted about 4:45 p.m. local time in Nishat Chowk, a market in Swat District's Mingora city, Swat Police Chief Qazi Muhammad Farooq told CNN. Pakistani military officials said the explosion was a suicide bomb attack on two military vehicles traveling through the market area. A Pakistani soldier was among the dead, they said. Half of the eight people killed were women, said Muhammad Ali, a staffer at Mingora Hospital. Another 45 were injured in the attack, he said. Mingora is the largest city in Pakistan's Swat Valley, the site of clashes between the Pakistani military and Islamic extremists. The Pakistani government has been trying to rout militants from their safe havens in the region.-- Journalist Nazar Ul Islam contributed to this report
